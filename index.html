Cerro Chirripó

It is the highest mountain in Costa Rica with an elevation of 3,820 meters (12.530 ft), It is located in Chirripó National Park and is noted for its ecological wealth. Location: San Gerardo de Rivas, Costa Rica - Distance: 21.5 kilometers (13.35 mi).

Corcovado National Park

It is the largest park in Costa Rica and encompasses an area of 424 square kilometers (164 sq mi) and it is 2.5% of the biodiversity of the word. National Geographic has called it "the most biologically intense place on Earth in terms of biodiversity". Location: Osa Peninsula, Costa Rica - Area: 424 square kilometers (164 sq mi).

Arenal Volcano

It is an active andesitic stratovolcano in north-western Costa Rica, measures at least 1,633 meters (5,358 ft) high, it is conically shaped with a crater 140 meters (460 ft) in diameter. Geologically, Arenal is considered a young volcano and it is estimated to be less than 7,500 years old. Location: La Fortuna de Alajuela, Costa Rica - Area: 32 square kilometers (19.88 sq mi)

Cocos Island

It is an island designated as a National Park with an area of approximately 23.85 square kilometers (9.21 sq mi) and a perimeter of around 23.3 km (14.5 mi) The island has 235 known species of flowering plants, of which 70, or nearly 30%, are endemic, has over 400 known species of insects, of which 65 (16%) are endemic and nearly 90 bird species have been reported. Nevertheless, more exhaustive investigations are expected to reveal many more species. Location: approximately 550 km (342 mi; 297 nmi) from the Pacific shore of Costa Rica

Nauyaca Waterfalls

Hidden among the mountains of Central Pacicfi, locaed in a canyon approximately 80 meters wide with one of the falls is 45 meters high in free fall and the other 20 meters high in stepped fall, forming at the foot of it, a pool of a thousand square meters and 6 meters deep. Location: Dominical San Isidro PZ, Costa Rica - Distance: 2 kilometers (1.24 mi).

Barva Volcano

Its cone is crowned by a crater of steep walls, covered with exuberant vegetation and whose bottom is occupied by a lagoon of almost circular shape, called Lagoon of the Barva, 8 meters of depth and 70 meters of diameter and that maintains a temperature between 10 and 18oC. There are two other lagoons next to the Barva lagoon: Danta lagoon also of crater origin and the Copey lagoon of marshy origin. The surface of the Barva volcano covers 1120 to 1500 square kilometers (695.93 to 932.05 mi), which makes it the largest volcano in Central America in terms of its extension. Location:Barva Heredia, Costa Rica. - Distance 3 kilometers (1.86 mi)

Manuel Antonio Beach

It is smallest national park, the diversity of wildlife in its 6.83 square kilometers (3 sq mi) is unequaled with 109 species of mammals and 184 species of birds. This park has one of the most impressive landscapes of Costa Rica and the world, has several inlets with multiple white sand beaches and lush foliage in the middle of large mountains and forests that reach the beaches, is in the forest life zone tropical wet. Location: Quepos Puntarenas, Costa Rica - Area: 1,983 ha (7.66 sq mi).

Cerro Dantas

The Cerro Dantas Wildlife Refuge located at the SW borders of the Braulio Carrillo National Park, with 57 hectares, the refuge has primarily secondary and primary cloud forests. Location: Monte de la Cruz Heredia, Costa Rica - Distance: 3.5 kilometers (2.17 mi).
